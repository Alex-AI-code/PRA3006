Aminoglutethimide

Used to inhibit the processes that produce steroids in patients with cancer that is sensitive to those hormones. This can decrease the rate of growth of cancer cells. It is used to treat prostate cancer in men and breast cancer only in women who have experienced menopause. This drug has been made obsolete by newer agents with better efficacy and fewer side effects.

Anastrozole

Used alongside other treatments such as radiation therapy or surgery to treat breast cancer in post – menopausal women. It inhibits aromatase, a key enzyme in the production of the estrogen hormone, decreasing the growth rate of hormone-sensitive cancer cells.

Dexrazoxane

A drug used to protect chemotherapy patients from toxic side effects to the heart.

Diethylstilbestrol

A synthetic form of estrogen previously used as hormonal therapy for breast and prostate cancer. It rarely used in current treatments, as it causes many health defects such as infertility, miscarriage, ectopic pregnancy, preeclampsia, preterm birth, stillbirth, infant death, menopause prior to age 45, breast cancer, cervical cancer, and vaginal cancer.

Ethinylestradiol

Estrogen medication. Wikidata displays Ethinylestradiol at two daily dosages: 25 micrograms and 1.5 milligrams. At 25 micrograms, this drug is more commonly used as a component of birth control. At 1.5 milligrams, this drug can used to improve the quality of life of patients with hormone-sensitive breast or prostate cancer.

A drug used in the early stages of breast cancer in post-menopausal women whose condition worsened after extended treatments with Tamixofen. It inhibits aromatase, a key enzyme in the production of the estrogen hormone, decreasing the growth rate of hormone-sensitive cancer cells.

Fulvestrant

Treats hormone-sensitive breast cancer in post – menopausal women; it is given by injection. This hormone binds to estrogen receptors found within cells, causing the receptor to be degraded and inhibiting the estrogen pathway of a cancerous cell.

Letrozole

Used to treat breast cancer in post-menopausal women that have already undergone prior therapy, such as surgery, radiation therapy, or Tamoxifen. It inhibits aromatase, a key enzyme in the production of the estrogen hormone, decreasing the growth rate of hormone-sensitive cancer cells.

Methyltestosterone

An anabolic steroid imitating testosterone and used to treat patients with breast cancer by inhibiting the release of estrogen, inhibiting the growth of hormone-sensitive cancers. Side effects can include masculinization symptoms, such as acne, hair growth and voice changes.

Read more...
Methotrexate

A chemotherapy agent used for numerous types of cancer, including breast cancer. It inhibits an enzyme responsible for synthesizing a folic acid, a component required for the synthesis of the nucleotide thymine. Without this nucleotide, the synthesis of DNA and RNA is inhibited, affecting all cellular functions.

Read more...
Sargramostim

A synthetic version of a signaling molecule used to induce the production of immune cells (It is a protein). As a side effect of chemotherapy, cancer patients can have a weakened immune system. This drug is used in an attempt to increase abnormally low concentrations of neutrophils, the most common white blood cell.

Read more...
Tamoxifen

A drug used to treat both early and advanced breast cancer over multiple years (specifically estrogen sensitive variants). Serious side effects include a small increased risk of uterine cancer, stroke, vision problems, and blood clots. It may also be used to prevent breast cancer in women that may be at high risk.

Read more...
Toremifene

Chemically related to Tamoxifen, this drug binds to estrogen receptors found within cells, causing the receptor to be degraded and inhibiting the estrogen pathway of a cancerous cell.
